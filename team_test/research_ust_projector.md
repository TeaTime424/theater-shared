## Why UST Was Rejected

### 1. AT Screen Incompatibility (Primary Blocker)

Option D places LCR speakers (3× Titan-815LX) behind an acoustically transparent screen. UST projectors project at a steep angle from below the screen. Standard AT screens (microperf, woven scatter) have fundamental problems with UST:

- **ALR/CLR screens** (designed for UST) reflect steep-angle light forward — but are NOT acoustically transparent. These are rigid, solid surfaces.
- **Standard AT screens** scatter light uniformly — UST light exits upward toward the ceiling, not toward the audience. Result: dim, washed-out image.
- **Moire artifacts** at steep UST incidence angles through AT perforations/weave.

### 2. UST-Compatible AT Screens Exist But Are Expensive

| Screen | UST+AT? | Gain | Sound Loss | Price (170" est.) |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Screen Excellence Visionaire USTAT | Purpose-built for UST+AT | ~0.8-1.0 | 6 dB @ 18kHz | $5,000+ (dealer quote) |
| Screen Excellence Enlightor-Neo | Woven scatter, works at any angle | 0.8 | 1.5 dB broadband | $3,000-6,000 |
| Stewart Microperf X² | Usable with care in dark room | 0.7 | Moderate | $4,000-8,000 |

These screens require a fully dark room (no ambient light rejection), have lower gain than standard AT, and cost 3-5× the budget ($1,500) for a conventional AT screen.

### 3. Contrast Ratio Gap

| | AWOL Aetherion Max | JVC DLA-NZ500 |
|--|-------------------|---------------|
| Native contrast | 6,000:1 | 40,000:1 |
| Delta | — | **6.7× better** |

In a dedicated dark theater, native contrast dominates perceived image quality. The JVC's D-ILA black levels are in a different class. The Aetherion's brightness advantage (3,300 vs 1,900 lumens) is irrelevant in a light-controlled room.

### 4. Total Cost Comparison

| Configuration | Projector | Screen | Total | Contrast |
|--------------|-----------|--------|-------|----------|
| JVC NZ500 + standard AT | $5,999 | $1,500 | **$7,499** | 40,000:1 |
| Aetherion Max + Visionaire USTAT | $4,499 | ~$5,000+ | **$9,499+** | 6,000:1 |
| Aetherion Max + Enlightor-Neo | $4,499 | ~$4,000 | **$8,499** | 6,000:1 |

UST costs MORE and delivers WORSE contrast. The only advantage — eliminating the ceiling mount at 17+ ft — doesn't justify a $1,000-2,000 premium with 6.7× worse black levels.

### 5. What UST Would Win On (Acknowledged)

- Eliminates throw distance constraint (NZ500 needs 17.3+ ft, tight in 23' room)
- 1ms input lag @ 240Hz (gaming)
- Dolby Vision HDR (NZ500 lacks this)
- Simpler installation (no ceiling mount)
- Brighter image (ambient light scenarios)

None of these override the AT screen incompatibility or contrast gap for a dedicated dark theater.

## Decision

**UST projectors are incompatible with Option D's front stage design (LCR behind AT screen).** Retain JVC DLA-NZ500 (or 2027 successor) with rear-wall ceiling mount at 17.3+ ft from screen surface.

If the design ever drops the AT screen requirement (visible LCR placement), UST becomes viable and should be reassessed at that time.

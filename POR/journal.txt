## 2025-12-15: Amplifier Standardization - Crown XLS 2502 (CORRECTED)

### Session Summary
Finalized amplifier selection for both main subwoofers and nearfield tactile platform. Standardized on Crown XLS 2502 across both systems for consistency, US warranty support, and proven reliability.

**CORRECTION:** Initial analysis incorrectly assumed dual 4Ω voice coils. Spec sheet confirms **dual 2Ω voice coils** for both UMII18-22 and UMII10-22. This changes the impedance calculations.

### Main Subwoofers (4× Dual UMII18-22)

**Final Selection:** 4× Crown XLS 2502 (bridged @ 8Ω)
- Power: 1,550W per cabinet, 775W per driver
- Total: 6,200W RMS
- Cost: $3,560 (4× $890)

**Wiring Configuration (CORRECTED):**
- Voice coils: Series (2Ω + 2Ω = 4Ω per driver)
- Drivers: Series (4Ω + 4Ω = 8Ω per cabinet)
- Load: 8Ω - required because Crown bridged min = 4Ω, parallel would be 2Ω

**Why not parallel drivers?**
- Parallel: 4Ω || 4Ω = 2Ω per cabinet
- Crown XLS 2502 bridged minimum = 4Ω
- 2Ω below bridged minimum — WON'T WORK
- Series: 4Ω + 4Ω = 8Ω — within spec

**Key Decision Factors:**
- 2× XLS 2502 stereo @ 2Ω = 2,400W (600W/driver) - meets target
- 4× XLS 2502 bridged @ 8Ω = 6,200W (775W/driver) - SELECTED (+0.9 dB headroom)
- Target was 625W/driver — 775W exceeds by +0.9 dB

### Nearfield Tactile Platform (8× UMII10-22)

**Final Selection:** 2× Crown XLS 2502 (stereo @ 2Ω)
- Power: 1,200W per channel, 4,800W total
- Per driver: 600W (2 drivers parallel per channel)
- Cost: $1,780

**Wiring Configuration:**
- Voice coils: Series (2Ω + 2Ω = 4Ω per driver)
- Drivers: Parallel per channel (4Ω || 4Ω = 2Ω)
- Crown stereo @ 2Ω = 1,200W/ch — within spec (2Ω stereo minimum)

## 2025-11-26: Rear Vertical Subwoofer Design - Volume Correction & Documentation

### Session Summary
Corrected the rear vertical subwoofer enclosure design to achieve ~12.5 ft³ gross internal volume (targeting ~12 ft³ net after displacement). This required increasing external depth from 19" to 22".

### Problem Statement
Initial design (v4.0) had 10.55 ft³ gross volume. User identified that after accounting for displacement from:
- Window braces (~0.30 ft³)
- 2× 18" drivers (~0.35 ft³)  
- Wiring/electronics (~0.20 ft³)
- **Total displacement: ~0.85 ft³**

Net volume would be only ~9.7 ft³, below target. Solution: increase depth to achieve 12.5 ft³ gross → ~11.5-12 ft³ net.

### Calculation Method
```
Target gross volume: 12.5 ft³ = 21,600 in³
Internal W × H fixed: 28.5" × 46.5" = 1,325.25 in²
Required depth: 21,600 ÷ 1,325.25 = 16.30"

External depth = sealed_depth + back_panel + baffle + recess
               = 16.75" + 0.75" + 1.5" + 3.0" = 22"

Actual volume: 28.5 × 46.5 × 16.75 = 22,198 in³ = 12.85 ft³ ✓
```

## METHODOLOGY NOTES (For Future Iteration)

### Subwoofer Enclosure Design Workflow

**1. Volume Calculation Chain**
```
Target NET volume (acoustic requirement)
  ↓ + displacement allowance (braces, drivers, wiring)
= Target GROSS volume
  ↓ ÷ (internal_W × internal_H)
= Required sealed DEPTH
  ↓ + back_panel + baffle_thickness + recess
= External DEPTH
```

**2. Displacement Estimates (for sealed 18" dual-driver enclosure)**
- Window braces (5 total): ~0.25-0.35 ft³
- 18" driver (each): ~0.15-0.20 ft³
- Wiring/terminals: ~0.15-0.25 ft³
- **Rule of thumb: ~1.0 ft³ total for dual-18" sealed**

### Nearfield Tactile System - Amplifier Selection Update

**Summary:** Revised amplifier recommendation from NX6000D ($500) to NX6000 ($433) after recognizing that Dayton DSP-408 handles all signal processing, making the NX6000D's built-in DSP redundant.

**Key Decision:**
- DSP-408 centralized architecture: 4 inputs from AV10 → outputs 1-4 to corner subs, outputs 5-6 to nearfield

## 2025-12-22: Nearfield Enclosure Optimization

### Session Summary

Analyzed nearfield enclosure sizing and identified significant free performance gains by using larger enclosures with heavy polyfill stuffing.

### Key Insight: Bigger Boxes = Lower Fc = Less EQ = More Headroom

For sealed enclosures, larger volume lowers Fc and reduces the EQ boost required to hit 15 Hz target. Heavy polyfill stuffing adds ~35-40% virtual volume.

### Front Nearfield (Riser-Mounted)

**Old Plan:** 8 cu ft enclosures
**New Plan:** 16 cu ft physical + heavy polyfill = ~22 cu ft virtual

The riser is 8 feet deep — enclosures only use 34" of 96" available.

| Metric | Old (8 cu ft) | New (16 cu ft stuffed) |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|
| Fc | 39 Hz | **30 Hz** |
| Qtc | 0.95 | **0.71** (optimal) |
| EQ @ 15 Hz | +16.5 dB | **+11 dB** |
| Headroom @ 15 Hz | Baseline | **+5 dB** |

### Rear Nearfield (Console Tower)

**Old Plan:** Console table ON platform (14 cu ft, 36" tall)
**New Plan:** Floor-standing tower TO floor (25 cu ft physical + polyfill = ~34 cu ft virtual)

Extending to floor adds 24" of free height.

| Metric | Old (on platform) | New (to floor, stuffed) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Fc | 31 Hz | **27 Hz** |
| Qtc | 0.74 | **0.65** |
| EQ @ 15 Hz | +13 dB | **+9 dB** |
| Headroom @ 15 Hz | +4 dB | **+7 dB** |

### Cost Delta

| Item | Old | New | Delta |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Front nearfield (materials) | $400 | $450 | +$50 |
| Rear nearfield (materials) | $350 | $550 | +$200 |
| **Total** | $750 | $1,000 | **+$250** |

**Cost per dB gained:** ~$40/dB — extremely cheap.

### Xmax Assessment

Confirmed 28mm Xmax is NOT a limiting factor for larger enclosures:
- Larger box = lower excursion demand at 15 Hz
- Driver works more efficiently, reaches closer to displacement-limited max
- 16 cu ft stuffed (Qtc 0.71) is near-optimal for UMII18-22
- 25 cu ft stuffed (Qtc 0.65) is slightly overdamped but excellent extension

### Updated Budget

LF System total: $11,799 → **$12,049** (+$250)

## 2025-12-22: Triangular Corner Column Subwoofers (Rev 5.9)

### Design Evolution

Evolved the corner-loaded main subwoofers from rectangular 8 cu ft boxes to integrated triangular corner columns. Two 4' cabinets per corner (split-build, joined-install) create floor-to-ceiling architectural elements that double as acoustic treatment.

### Critical Insight: Maintain Corner Loading

Initial triangular design placed drivers at 24"/72" heights, which reduced corner loading by 3-4 dB. Solution: Mount BOTH drivers in the BOTTOM cabinet at 11"/32" (identical to old rectangular design), with TOP cabinet providing volume extension only. This maintains full +10-12 dB corner loading gain.

### Key Design Decisions

1. **Triangular cross-section** — fits corners perfectly, inherently rigid
2. **24" × 24" × 48" internal per cabinet** — combined yields 10.25 cu ft virtual per driver
3. **Split-build, joined-install** — easier handling (130-180 lbs vs 310 lb monolith)
4. **Both drivers in bottom cabinet** — maintains corner loading (11"/32" from floor)
5. **Battle-ready dual seal** — butyl rope + internal batten + 10 bolts
6. **2" OC 703 face with driver cutouts** — integrated absorption, hides joint
7. **AT fabric wrap** — looks like acoustic panels, not subwoofers

## 2025-12-22: Platform-Penetrating Rear Corner Design (Rev 5.9 Refinement)

### The Problem

Original split-build design placed both drivers in the bottom cabinet at 11"/32" from floor. For rear corners behind the 24" platform, this creates issues:
- Lower driver at 11" would be enclosed under platform
- No air path for displacement
- Corner loading compromised

### The Elegant Solution

Full-height 96" columns PENETRATE through the platform. Platform has triangular cutouts for sub columns. Platform acts as acoustic boundary for corner loading.

**Key Insight:** At 20 Hz, a solid platform (2×10 joists + 3/4" plywood) is acoustically massive and acts as a reflective boundary — the driver doesn't "know" there's air underneath.

### Configuration Comparison

| Parameter | Front Corners | Rear Corners |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Column height | 96" | 96" |
| Bottom cabinet | Both drivers (11"/32") | Single driver at TOP (27") |
| Top cabinet | Volume only | Single driver at BOTTOM (51") |
| Corner boundary | Floor (0") | Platform (24") |
| Volume per driver | 10.25 cu ft | 10.25 cu ft |
| Fc / Qtc | 30 Hz / 0.72 | 30 Hz / 0.72 |
| Corner loading | +10-12 dB | +10-12 dB |

### Why This Works

1. **Full volume preserved** — 96" column = same 10.25 cu ft per driver
2. **Platform as boundary** — Solid construction acts as acoustic floor at 20 Hz
3. **Corner loading maintained** — Lower driver 3" above platform = excellent coupling
4. **Solid platform** — No gaps needed, just triangular cutouts for penetration
5. **Modal distribution bonus** — Different front/rear driver heights may improve uniformity

### Platform Requirements

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Cutout size | ~28" × 28" right triangle (per corner) |
| Joists | 2×10 @ 16" OC minimum |
| Deck | 3/4" plywood (double layer at corners) |
| Corner blocking | Double joists around cutouts |
| Point load | 400+ lbs per corner |

- **Baffle is DOUBLE-LAYER (1.5" thick)** - laminate two 0.75" panels together
- **Cut driver holes in BOTH baffle layers BEFORE laminating**
- **Drivers are recessed 3" from front edge** (baffle mounts on cleats)
- **Voice coils wired in SERIES:** 2Ω + 2Ω = 4Ω per driver
- **Two drivers in SERIES:** 4Ω + 4Ω = 8Ω cabinet impedance

# BUILD SEQUENCE

## WEEK 1: Panel Preparation

### Step 1: Cut All Panels (1.5 hours)

Cut panels per cut list. Label each piece clearly.

- 1× Back panel (23.5" × 44.0")
- 2× Side panels (17.75" × 44.0")
- 1× Top panel (22.0" × 17.75")
- 1× Bottom panel (22.0" × 17.75")
- **2× Baffle layers (22.0" × 42.5")**

### Step 2: Cut Driver Holes in BOTH Baffle Layers (1 hour)

**CRITICAL: Cut matching holes in BOTH baffle layers before laminating.**

**Procedure:**
1. Stack both baffle panels, clamp together
2. Mark center points on top panel:
   - Top driver: 31.0" from bottom, 11.0" from sides
   - Bottom driver: 10.0" from bottom, 11.0" from sides
3. Use router with circle jig for clean 16.875" diameter cuts
4. Cut through BOTH layers simultaneously, or cut separately using same template
5. Sand edges smooth
6. Verify both panels have identical cutouts

### Step 3: Drill Bolt Holes in BOTH Baffle Layers (30 min)

Drill 8 bolt holes per driver (16 total per layer, 32 holes total).

**Bolt Circle:**
- Diameter: 17.75"
- Holes: 8 per driver, evenly spaced (45° apart)
- Hole size: 5/16" (for 1/4-20 T-nuts)

**Procedure:**
1. Create a drilling template from 1/4" hardboard
2. Use template on BOTH baffle layers
3. Verify holes align when layers are stacked

### Step 4: Laminate Baffle Layers (1 hour + 24hr cure)

**This creates the 1.5" thick baffle assembly.**

**Procedure:**
1. Dry-fit both layers, verify all holes align
2. Insert temporary bolts through 4 corner holes to maintain alignment
3. Apply generous wood glue to mating face
4. Stack layers, press together
5. Install remaining temporary bolts through driver holes
6. Clamp thoroughly around perimeter and between cutouts
7. Use cauls (flat boards) to distribute pressure evenly
8. Wipe off squeeze-out
9. **Let cure 24 hours before removing clamps**

### Step 5: Install T-Nuts (20 min)

Install T-nuts from the BACK of the laminated baffle (the face that goes inside cabinet).

- 16 T-nuts total (8 per driver)
- 1/4-20 thread
- Tap in with hammer
- Apply drop of CA glue to secure
- T-nuts have full 1.5" of thread engagement

### Step 6: Cut Bracing (45 min)

**Cross-Braces:**
- 2× Width braces: 20.5" × 4.0" × 0.75" (center notch 2"×2")
- 2× Depth braces: 10.25" × 4.0" × 0.75" (center notch 2"×2")

**Rib Braces:**
- 6× ribs: 1.5" × 30" × 0.75"

**Corner Braces:**
- 4× L-shaped: 3" × 42.5" × 0.75"

**Cleats:**
- 2× Side cleats: 0.75" × 0.75" × 42.5"
- 2× Top/bottom cleats: 0.75" × 0.75" × 20.5"

---

## WEEK 2: Cabinet Assembly

### Step 7: Seal Interior Surfaces (1 hour + dry time)

Seal ALL interior-facing surfaces with thinned Titebond III (10:1 glue:water).

**Surfaces to seal:**
- Inside faces of all cabinet panels
- Back face of laminated baffle
- All faces of bracing pieces

Let dry 4+ hours or overnight.

### Step 8: Cut Terminal Plate Hole (15 min)

**Location:** Back panel, centered horizontally, 22" from bottom  
**Cutout:** ~3.5" × 2.5" (verify with your terminal plate)

### Step 9: Install Rib Braces on Panels (30 min)

Attach rib braces to side panels and back panel BEFORE assembly.

**Side Panel Rib Positions:**
- 2 ribs per side: 6" and 12" from back edge
- Centered vertically

**Back Panel Rib Positions:**
- 2 ribs: 7" from each side edge
- Centered vertically

**Attachment:** Glue + screws (#8 × 1.5")

### Step 10: Install Baffle Support Cleats (20 min)

Glue cleats to side/top/bottom panels at baffle position.

**Cleat Position: 4.5" from front edge of each panel**

This positions the 1.5" baffle so its front face is 3" back from cabinet front:
- 4.5" (cleat position) - 1.5" (baffle thickness) = 3" recess

### Step 11: Assemble Back and Sides (1 hour)

Join the two side panels to the back panel.

**Procedure:**
1. Apply glue to back panel edges
2. Attach side panels with screws (SPAX #8 × 2")
3. Screws every 4-5" along joint
4. **Check for square** - measure diagonals (should be equal)
5. Clamp and let glue set (1 hour minimum)

### Step 12: Install Cross-Braces (45 min)

Install interlocking cross-braces at TWO levels:
- Level 1: 14" from bottom
- Level 2: 28" from bottom

**Procedure:**
1. Mark brace positions on side panels
2. Interlock braces (notches engage)
3. Glue and screw to side panels
4. Verify braces are level

### Step 13: Install Corner Braces (20 min)

Glue corner braces into all 4 vertical corners.
- Full height (42.5")
- L-shaped profile
- Glue + screws

### Step 14: Attach Bottom Panel (20 min)

1. Apply glue to bottom edges of side/back panels
2. Position bottom panel (flush with outside)
3. Screw through bottom into side/back edges
4. SPAX #8 × 2" screws every 4"

### Step 15: Attach Top Panel (20 min)

Same procedure as bottom panel.

### Step 16: Install Front Baffle (30 min)

1. Test fit laminated baffle - should rest on cleats
2. Apply glue to cleats and cabinet edges
3. Position baffle on cleats (front face 3" back from cabinet front)
4. Screw through baffle perimeter into cleats and cabinet edges
5. Use SPAX #8 × 2" screws every 4" around perimeter

### Step 17: Seal Interior Joints (30 min)

Run silicone caulk bead along ALL interior joints:
- All panel-to-panel joints
- Around baffle edges
- Around cross-braces

Smooth with finger. **Let cure 24 hours.**

---

## WEEK 3: Finishing & Final Assembly

### Step 18: Route Edges (30 min)

Use 1/4" roundover bit on all external edges:
- All vertical corners
- Top edges
- Bottom edges
- Front recess edges (inside lip)

### Step 19: Final Sanding (30 min)

1. Sand all exterior surfaces - 120 grit
2. Fill any voids/screw holes with wood filler
3. Let dry, sand flush
4. Final sand - 150 grit
5. Wipe with tack cloth

### Step 20: Apply Finish (1 hour + dry time)

**ExoHyde Application:**
1. Stir thoroughly
2. Apply heavy coat with foam roller
3. Work in sections, including inside of recess
4. One coat usually sufficient
5. **Let cure 24+ hours**

### Step 21: Install Terminal Plate (10 min)

1. Apply gasket tape around cutout
2. Insert terminal plate from outside
3. Secure with screws
4. Attach internal wiring leads (leave slack)

### Step 22: Mount Drivers (30 min)

1. Apply foam gasket tape around each cutout
2. Run speaker wire to terminal plate
3. Connect wire to first driver
4. Lower driver into recess
5. Install bolts (1/4-20 × 2") finger-tight
6. Tighten in star pattern (don't over-torque)
7. Repeat for second driver

### Step 23: Wire Drivers in Series (15 min)

**Series Connection for 8Ω Total:**

```
Terminal (+) → Top Driver VC1(+) → VC1(-) → VC2(+) → VC2(-)
                                    ↓
            Bottom Driver VC1(+) → VC1(-) → VC2(+) → VC2(-)
                                                        ↓
Terminal (-)
```

**Each Driver:**
- Wire both voice coils in SERIES: 2Ω + 2Ω = 4Ω per driver

**Both Drivers:**
- Wire in SERIES: 4Ω + 4Ω = 8Ω cabinet impedance

**Result:** Both drivers move OUT together (in phase)

### Step 24: Test (15 min)

1. Connect to amplifier (LOW volume)
2. Play test tone (50 Hz)
3. Verify both drivers move in same direction (out together)
4. Check for air leaks (feel around joints)
5. Gradually increase volume
6. Listen for rattles or buzzes

# SAFETY NOTES & TIPS

## Safety Notes

- Always wear safety glasses when cutting or drilling
- Use hearing protection when operating power tools
- Ensure adequate ventilation when using adhesives or finishes
- Keep workspace clean and organized
- Use sharp blades for cleaner cuts
- Support large panels properly when cutting
- Clamp work pieces securely before drilling or routing
- Never exceed driver's Xmax (28mm) during testing
- Allow driver to cool between high-power tests

## Construction Tips

- **Laminating baffle:** Use temporary bolts through driver holes to maintain perfect alignment
- **Cutting circles:** Router circle jig provides cleaner cuts than jigsaw
- **Square check:** Measure both diagonals - should be equal when box is square
- **Glue squeeze-out:** Wipe excess immediately with damp cloth
- **Screw pilot holes:** Predrill to prevent splitting, especially near edges
- **Bracing placement:** Position cross-braces between driver locations
- **Air leaks:** Run bead of caulk along all internal seams for airtight seal
- **Terminal location:** Center of back panel, accessible height (~22" from bottom)
- **Driver mounting:** Tighten bolts in star pattern to prevent warping baffle
- **Polyfill:** Medium density works best - should fill space but not compress

## Troubleshooting

- **Cabinet rattles:** Check all screws tight, verify bracing secure, seal air leaks
- **Driver rub:** Voice coil hitting - check driver centered in cutout, verify no damage
- **Low output:** Check wiring polarity, verify series configuration, check for air leaks
- **Panel resonance:** Add more bracing or damping material if needed

# NEXT STEPS: 100-HOUR BURN-IN & TESTING

After construction is complete, proceed to verification testing:

## 1. 100-Hour Burn-In Protocol (before measurements)

- **Hours 0-10:** 30-80 Hz sweep @ 40% power (~300W)
- **Hours 10-50:** 20-80 Hz sweep @ 60% power (~500W)
- **Hours 50-100:** 20-80 Hz sweep @ 70% power (~600W)
- **Check every 10 hours:** visual inspection, temps, mechanical noises

## 2. Rest Period: 24 hours after burn-in

## 3. REW Measurements

### Configuration A (empty):
- Impedance sweep
- Nearfield response
- Ground plane @ 1m
- SPL capability
- Distortion measurement

### Configuration B (stuffed with polyfill):
- Repeat all tests
- Calculate virtual volume multiplier
- Compare to predictions

## 4. Data Analysis

- Compare measured vs predicted (WinISD)
- Verify driver T/S parameters
- Document amplifier derating at ULF
- Calculate virtual volume multiplier

## 5. Go/No-Go Decision

**✅ All tests pass** → Proceed with 16-driver system  
**⚠️ Minor issues** → Modify design and proceed  
**❌ Major problems** → Abort/redesign

# QUICK REFERENCE FORMULAS

## Calculate Power from SPL

```
Power (W) = 2.83² × 10^((SPL_measured - Sensitivity)/10)

Where:
- SPL_measured = dB measured at 1m
- Sensitivity = 90.7 dB @ 2.83V/1m (UMII18-22)
- Result = Power in watts

Example:
SPL @ 1m = 105 dB
Power = 8 × 10^((105-90.7)/10) = 8 × 10^1.43 = ~215W
```

## Calculate Virtual Volume from Fc Shift

```
Virtual_Volume = Physical_Volume × (Fc_empty / Fc_stuffed)²

Where:
- Fc_empty = measured resonance without polyfill
- Fc_stuffed = measured resonance with polyfill
- Result = effective volume in same units as physical

Example:
Fc_empty = 39.4 Hz, Fc_stuffed = 35.7 Hz
Virtual = 7.95 × (39.4/35.7)² = 7.95 × 1.22 = 9.7 ft³
Multiplier = 9.7 / 7.95 = 1.22×
```

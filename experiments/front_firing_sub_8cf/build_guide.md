# 8 cu ft Dual Front-Firing Subwoofer - Build Guide

## Overview

This guide covers construction of a dual front-firing 18" subwoofer using:
- 2× Dayton Audio UMII18-22 drivers
- 3/4" plywood construction
- **1.5" double-layer baffle** (two 0.75" layers laminated)
- 3" recessed baffle (baffle front face is 3" back from cabinet front)
- Enhanced bracing for vibration control

**Estimated Build Time:** 10-14 hours (excluding drying times)

---

## Tools Required

### Power Tools
- Circular saw or table saw
- Jigsaw (for driver cutouts)
- Router with 1/4" roundover bit and circle jig
- Drill/driver
- Random orbital sander

### Hand Tools
- Clamps (minimum 8 bar clamps for baffle lamination)
- Square
- Tape measure
- Pencil/marker
- Caulk gun

### Consumables
- 120 and 220 grit sandpaper
- Tack cloth
- Wood glue (Titebond III)
- Silicone caulk
- ExoHyde finish

---

## Build Sequence

### Phase 1: Panel Preparation (Day 1)

#### Step 1: Cut All Panels (1.5 hours)

Cut panels per cut list. Label each piece clearly.

**Main Panels:**
| Part | Qty | Dimensions |
|------|-----|------------|
| Back | 1 | 23.5" × 44.0" |
| Sides | 2 | 17.75" × 44.0" |
| Top | 1 | 22.0" × 17.75" |
| Bottom | 1 | 22.0" × 17.75" |
| **Baffle Layers** | **2** | **22.0" × 42.5"** |

**Cleats (baffle support):**
| Part | Qty | Dimensions |
|------|-----|------------|
| Side Cleats | 2 | 0.75" × 0.75" × 42.5" |
| Top/Bottom Cleats | 2 | 0.75" × 0.75" × 20.5" |

#### Step 2: Cut Driver Holes in BOTH Baffle Layers (1 hour)

**Critical:** Cut matching holes in BOTH baffle layers before laminating.

**Driver Cutout Specifications:**
```
Cutout diameter:      16.875"
Baffle dimensions:    22.0" × 42.5"
Top driver center:    31.0" from baffle bottom, 11.0" from sides
Bottom driver center: 10.0" from baffle bottom, 11.0" from sides
```

**Procedure:**
1. Stack both baffle panels, clamp together
2. Mark center points on top panel
3. Use router with circle jig for clean cuts, OR drill pilot hole and cut with jigsaw
4. Cut through BOTH layers simultaneously if possible, or cut separately using same template
5. Sand edges smooth
6. Verify both panels have identical cutouts

#### Step 3: Drill Bolt Holes in BOTH Baffle Layers (30 min)

Drill 8 bolt holes per driver (16 total per layer, 32 holes total).

**Bolt Circle:**
- Diameter: 17.75"
- Holes: 8 per driver, evenly spaced (45° apart)
- Hole size: 5/16" (for 1/4-20 T-nuts)

**Procedure:**
1. Create a drilling template from 1/4" hardboard
2. Use template on BOTH baffle layers
3. Verify holes align when layers are stacked

#### Step 4: Laminate Baffle Layers (1 hour + 24hr cure)

**This creates the 1.5" thick baffle assembly.**

**Procedure:**
1. Dry-fit both layers, verify all holes align
2. Insert temporary bolts through 4 corner holes to maintain alignment
3. Apply generous wood glue to mating face
4. Stack layers, press together
5. Install remaining temporary bolts through driver holes
6. Clamp thoroughly around perimeter and between cutouts
7. Use cauls (flat boards) to distribute pressure evenly
8. Wipe off squeeze-out
9. Let cure 24 hours before removing clamps

```
BAFFLE CROSS-SECTION:

    ┌─────────────────────────┐
    │   Layer 1 (0.75")       │ ← Front (driver mounting face)
    ├─────────────────────────┤
    │   Layer 2 (0.75")       │ ← Back (inside cabinet)
    └─────────────────────────┘
          Total: 1.5"
```

#### Step 5: Install T-Nuts (20 min)

Install T-nuts from the BACK of the laminated baffle (the face that goes inside cabinet).

- 16 T-nuts total (8 per driver)
- 1/4-20 thread
- Tap in with hammer
- Apply drop of CA glue to secure
- T-nuts have full 1.5" of thread engagement

#### Step 6: Cut Bracing (45 min)

**Cross-Braces:**
| Qty | Dimensions | Notes |
|-----|------------|-------|
| 2 | 20.5" × 4.0" × 0.75" | Width braces, center notch |
| 2 | 10.25" × 4.0" × 0.75" | Depth braces, center notch |

Cut 2" wide × 2" deep notch at center of each brace for interlocking.

**Rib Braces:**
| Qty | Dimensions |
|-----|------------|
| 6 | 1.5" × 30" × 0.75" |

**Corner Braces:**
| Qty | Dimensions |
|-----|------------|
| 4 | 3" × 42.5" (L-shaped) |

---

### Phase 2: Sealing & Prep (Day 2)

#### Step 7: Seal Interior Surfaces (1 hour + dry time)

Seal ALL interior-facing surfaces with thinned Titebond III (10:1 glue:water).

**Surfaces to seal:**
- Inside faces of all cabinet panels (back, sides, top, bottom)
- Back face of laminated baffle
- All faces of bracing pieces

Let dry 4+ hours or overnight.

#### Step 8: Cut Terminal Plate Hole (15 min)

**Location:** Back panel, centered horizontally, 22" from bottom

**Cutout:** ~3.5" × 2.5" (verify with your terminal plate)

---

### Phase 3: Cabinet Assembly (Day 2-3)

#### Step 9: Install Rib Braces on Panels (30 min)

Attach rib braces to side panels and back panel BEFORE assembly.

**Side Panel Rib Positions:**
- 6" and 12" from back edge (in the sealed chamber area)
- Centered vertically

**Back Panel Rib Positions:**
- 7" from each side edge
- Centered vertically

**Attachment:** Glue + brad nails

#### Step 10: Install Baffle Support Cleats (20 min)

Glue cleats to side/top/bottom panels at baffle position.

**Cleat Position:** 4.5" from front edge of each panel

This positions the 1.5" baffle so its front face is 3" back from cabinet front:
- 4.5" (cleat position) - 1.5" (baffle thickness) = 3" recess

```
SIDE VIEW (cleat position):

    │←─ 3" ─→│←1.5"→│
    │        │baffle│
    ├────────┼──────┼─────────────────────┤
    │ recess │██████│   sealed chamber    │
    │        │██████│                     │
    │        │██████│                     │
    │  cleat→▓██████│                     │
    │        │██████│                     │
    ├────────┼──────┼─────────────────────┤
            4.5"
            from front
```

#### Step 11: Assemble Back and Sides (1 hour)

Join the two side panels to the back panel.

**Procedure:**
1. Apply glue to back panel edges
2. Attach side panels with screws (SPAX #8 × 2")
3. Screws every 4-5" along joint
4. Check for square — measure diagonals
5. Clamp and let glue set (1 hour minimum)

#### Step 12: Install Cross-Braces (45 min)

Install interlocking cross-braces at TWO levels:
- Level 1: 14" from bottom
- Level 2: 28" from bottom

**Procedure:**
1. Mark brace positions on side panels
2. Interlock braces (notches engage)
3. Glue and screw to side panels
4. Verify braces are level

#### Step 13: Install Corner Braces (20 min)

Glue corner braces into all 4 vertical corners.
- Full height (42.5")
- Glue + brad nails

#### Step 14: Attach Bottom Panel (20 min)

1. Apply glue to bottom edges of side/back panels
2. Position bottom panel (flush with outside)
3. Screw through bottom into side/back edges
4. SPAX #8 × 2" screws every 4"

#### Step 15: Attach Top Panel (20 min)

Same procedure as bottom panel.
Use shorter screws (SPAX #8 × 1.5") to avoid breakthrough.

#### Step 16: Install Front Baffle (30 min)

1. Test fit laminated baffle — should rest on cleats
2. Apply glue to cleats and cabinet edges
3. Position baffle on cleats (front face 3" back from cabinet front)
4. Screw through baffle perimeter into cleats and cabinet edges
5. Use SPAX #8 × 2.5" screws (longer due to 1.5" baffle)
6. Screws every 4" around perimeter

---

### Phase 4: Finishing (Day 3-4)

#### Step 17: Seal Interior Joints (30 min)

Run silicone caulk bead along ALL interior joints:
- All panel-to-panel joints
- Around baffle edges
- Around cross-braces

Smooth with finger. Let cure 24 hours.

#### Step 18: Route Edges (30 min)

Use 1/4" roundover bit on all external edges:
- All vertical corners
- Top edges
- Bottom edges
- Front recess edges (inside lip)

#### Step 19: Final Sanding (30 min)

1. Sand all exterior surfaces — 120 grit
2. Fill any voids/screw holes with wood filler
3. Let dry, sand flush
4. Final sand — 150 grit
5. Wipe with tack cloth

#### Step 20: Apply Finish (1 hour + dry time)

**ExoHyde Application:**
1. Stir thoroughly
2. Apply heavy coat with foam roller
3. Work in sections, including inside of recess
4. One coat usually sufficient
5. Let cure 24+ hours

---

### Phase 5: Final Assembly (Day 4)

#### Step 21: Install Terminal Plate (10 min)

1. Apply gasket tape around cutout
2. Insert terminal plate from outside
3. Secure with screws
4. Attach internal wiring leads (leave slack)

#### Step 22: Mount Drivers (30 min)

1. Apply foam gasket tape around each cutout
2. Run speaker wire to terminal plate
3. Connect wire to first driver
4. Lower driver into recess
5. Install bolts (1/4-20 × 2") finger-tight
6. Tighten in star pattern
7. Repeat for second driver

**Wiring (Series Connection):**
```
Terminal (+) ──→ Top Driver (+)
Top Driver (-) ──→ Bottom Driver (+)
Bottom Driver (-) ──→ Terminal (-)

Result: 4Ω total impedance
Both drivers move OUT together (in phase)
```

#### Step 23: Test (15 min)

1. Connect to amplifier (LOW volume)
2. Play test tone (50 Hz)
3. Verify both drivers move in same direction (out together)
4. Check for air leaks (feel around joints)
5. Gradually increase volume
6. Listen for rattles or buzzes

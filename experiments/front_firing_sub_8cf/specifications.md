The baffle is mounted 3" back from the front edges of the sides/top/bottom panels.
This creates a recessed "pocket" that provides:
- Protection for driver cones
- Aesthetic recessed appearance
- Space for grille mounting if desired
- NO laminated frame required - simpler construction

## Bracing System (Enhanced for Front-Firing)

**Critical:** Front-firing configuration has NO force cancellation. Both drivers push in the same direction, creating cabinet reaction forces. Bracing must be more robust than dual-opposed design.

### Cross-Braces (Interlocking)
- Quantity: 4 (2 per level)
- Dimensions: 22" × 4" × 0.75"
- Position: 14" and 28" from bottom (between drivers)
- Purpose: Stiffen side panels against pressure

### Rib Braces
- Quantity: 8 (2 per side panel + 2 on back)
- Dimensions: 1.5" × 32.5" × 0.75"
- Position: Evenly spaced
- Purpose: Break up panel resonances

### Corner Braces
- Quantity: 4 (full height)
- Dimensions: 3" × 3" L-profile, 42.5" tall
- Purpose: Reinforce plywood edge joints

### Baffle Reinforcement
- Baffle edges supported by cleats on sides/top/bottom
- Additional vertical stiffener between driver cutouts recommended

## Stability Considerations

| Metric | Value | Assessment |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Height/Width ratio | 1.87 | Moderate |
| Height/Depth ratio | 2.38 | Tallish |
| Footprint | 3.02 sq ft | Good base |
| CG location | Mid-height, toward front | Drivers are heavy |

### Stability Recommendations

1. **Weighted base:** Add steel plate or sand chamber in bottom if needed
2. **Spikes or cones:** Couple cabinet to floor, prevent walking
3. **Wall proximity:** Place near wall for additional stability
4. **Anti-tip:** Consider wall anchor for earthquake zones

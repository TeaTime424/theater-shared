## Critical Assessment

**Where money goes vs value:**
- **Building prep (37%)** is unavoidable — you can't skip framing, electrical, HVAC, and flooring. This is the cost of converting a garage to a theater. Good news: it adds real property value.
- **Projector + screen (19%)** is the single largest theater component. JVC NZ500 at $6K is entry-level for native 4K laser. No cheaper alternative delivers comparable contrast and resolution on a 170" AT screen.
- **Bass system (13%)** delivers 8x 18" sealed corner-loaded subs + 8 shakers for $5,325. That's $665 per 18" sub channel including amp and enclosure. Exceptional value for weapons-grade bass.
- **Processor + DSP (12%)** — X6800H at $3,700 replaces AV10 ($3,500) + external amp ($2,500+). Net savings of ~$2,300 by using internal amplification for 8 surround/height channels.

**Budget risks:**
- Phase 1 estimates are rough. Permits could run higher in Lee County, NC. Architectural plans at $1,000 assumes simple residential — if engineer stamps are required, add $500-1,000.
- $1,000 per couch is tight for 4-seat units. Quality used or IKEA-tier. Fine for a theater but manage expectations.
- Sub enclosure material cost of $125 per column assumes DIY plywood cuts and self-bracing. If shop-cut, add $50-75 per column.
- No contingency line item. Recommend 10% on Phase 1 ($1,491) held in reserve.

**What's not in the budget:**
- Acoustic treatment installation labor — materials are owned ($4,250 value) but install is listed at $0. This is DIY labor, not a cost gap.
- Calibration equipment — UMIK-1 owned, REW is free. No cost.
- Streaming/source devices — assumed owned or minimal cost.
- Any cosmetic upgrades (accent lighting, wall fabric, acoustic panel framing).

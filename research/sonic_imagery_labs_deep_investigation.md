## 7. How to Ask Technical Questions Before Purchasing

### Best options (ranked by likelihood of response):

1. **eBay message via the 898B listing** — Richard has a proven track record of responding to eBay buyer questions. Multiple feedback entries confirm "answered questions immediately." The Reverb "Quick Responder" badge corroborates this pattern.

2. **Email: nerdman@ix.netcom.com** — Listed on every page of the website. Note: ix.netcom.com is an old AT&T/Netcom email domain. The Dagogo reviewer may have used a different contact method, or the email was filtered/missed.

3. **Phone: 510-728-1146** — Listed on every page. Unknown whether this is a cell, landline, or goes to voicemail. No hours of operation published.

4. **Reverb message** — "Quick Responder" badge earned on Reverb suggests messages are answered promptly.

### What to ask about 0.9V input:
The 898B is spec'd for -10 dBV input (0.316 Vrms nominal). Your ADAU1701 outputs ~0.9 Vrms, which is 2.85x the nominal input — about +9 dBV above the design center. Key questions:
- "Will a 0.9 Vrms input (higher than -10 dBV nominal) cause clipping or distortion?"
- "What is the maximum input voltage before clipping on the unbalanced side?"
- "At 0.9 Vrms input, what output voltage should I expect on the balanced side?"

The max output is +22.3 dBu (~10.1 Vrms), so headroom exists. But confirming the input stage can handle 0.9V cleanly is worth asking.

The [Dagogo article](https://www.dagogo.com/audio-blast-return-discrete-opamp-roller/) by Doug Schroeder states:

The article lists SIL's op-amp specifications alongside Sparkos and Burson, but **only provides listening impressions for Sparkos and Burson products**. SIL's products are conspicuously absent from the hands-on portion.

The article does NOT explicitly state "never got a response" in the text I was able to access. However, the pattern is clear: Schroeder contacted Sparkos and Burson (both provided units for review), while SIL products were listed spec-only with no listening impressions — strongly implying SIL either didn't respond to the review request or declined to participate.

**This is not necessarily about being unresponsive to customers** — declining/ignoring a review request from a publication is common for small companies that don't have spare review units.

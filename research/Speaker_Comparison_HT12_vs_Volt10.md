## Why NOT HT-12?

**HT-12 is optimized for different application:**
- Designed as **LCR speaker** (behind screen duty)
- Shallow 9.5" depth critical for behind-screen mounting
- Ported design for extended bass response
- Traditional woofer + compression driver (not coaxial)

**But in this theater:**
- ❌ Already have reference LCR (Titan-815LX)
- ❌ Don't need behind-screen optimization for surrounds
- ❌ Ported design irrelevant (80 Hz crossover to subwoofers)
- ❌ Equivalent sensitivity (both ~95 dB)
- ❌ More expensive ($289 vs $242)
- ❌ Less optimal for off-axis Atmos/surround duty

---

## What is Coaxial Design?

**Volt-10 Configuration:**
- 10" Denovo Audio CX-10 coaxial woofer (custom Eminence Beta-10CX)
- Celestion CDX1-1446 compression driver (custom) mounts to **back** of woofer
- Sound fires **through small waveguide in center** of woofer cone
- Result: **Point source** - all frequencies from same acoustic point

**Advantages for Atmos/Surrounds:**
- Better off-axis response (no separate acoustic centers)
- More consistent sound across different listening positions
- Superior phase coherence (all frequencies time-aligned)
- Ideal for ceiling mounting (Row 1 and Row 2 hear consistent sound)
- Better coverage of wide listening area

**vs. Traditional Design (HT-12):**
- Woofer and compression driver physically separated
- Different acoustic centers create path-length differences off-axis
- Good for on-axis LCR duty, less optimal for off-axis surround/Atmos

## Addendum A: DIYSG Titan-815LX Specifications

**Purpose:** Document LCR speaker specifications for amplifier matching and system integration.

### Official Specifications

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Sensitivity** | 99 dB @ 2.83V/1m | DIYSG product page |
| **Impedance** | 6Ω nominal | Matt Grant (HiFi Circuit) |
| **Minimum Impedance** | 4.2Ω @ 110 Hz | Matt Grant (HiFi Circuit) |
| **Power Handling** | 400W (with 80 Hz crossover) | Matt Grant (HiFi Circuit) |
| **Cabinet Tuning** | 40 Hz (ported) | Matt Grant (HiFi Circuit) |
| **Recommended Crossover** | 60-80 Hz | Matt Grant |
| **Minimum Crossover** | 40 Hz (with good room gain) | Matt Grant |

### Source Citation

From HiFi Circuit forum post by Matt Grant (DIYSG designer), August 5, 2023:
> "Essentially the Titan-815 uses the larger 8" midrange / HF horn from the Titan-818 and combines it with a 15" cabinet same as used on the original Titan-615. [...] Tuning in the stock cabinet is 40Hz so if you have good room gain it's possible to get away with a 40Hz crossover [...] With a 80hz crossover it would be good for 400w before you would see a benefit moving to the larger Titan-818."

Thread: http://www.hificircuit.com/community/threads/anything-on-the-815xl-anything-like-comparison-to-the-615xl-or-818xl.5944/

### Amplifier Requirements

**For 40 Hz crossover (this system):**

| Amplifier | Power @ 4Ω | Power @ 6Ω (est.) | SPL @ 1m | SPL @ 13 ft | vs THX |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|----------|-------------|--------|
| NC502MP | 500W | ~400W | 125 dB | 111 dB | +6 dB |
| NC252MP | 250W | ~200W | 122 dB | 108 dB | +3 dB |

**Decision:** NC252MP provides adequate +3-4 dB headroom above THX Reference for LCR channels with 40 Hz crossover.

### Impedance Considerations

- **Minimum impedance (4.2Ω) occurs at 110 Hz** - within passband regardless of crossover setting
- NC252MP rated for 4Ω operation - safe with Titan-815LX minimum impedance
- 40 Hz crossover increases thermal load vs 80 Hz but remains within amplifier capability

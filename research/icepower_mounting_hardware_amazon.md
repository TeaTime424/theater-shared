## Executive Summary

The ICEpower 1200AS2 baseplate has 18x M3 threaded holes, 5mm thread depth, 0.5 Nm max torque. Mounting to an aluminum plate requires drilling 3.2–3.4mm clearance holes through the aluminum, then screwing M3 machine screws down into the module's existing threads. With 5 modules at ~18 holes each you need ~90–100 screws. Four Amazon product categories cover the full job: M3 screws, M3 washers, a metric drill bit set, and thermal compound.

---

## Screw Length Selection: M3 x 4mm vs M3 x 5mm

**Critical constraint:** Module thread depth = 5mm max. Screw must pass through aluminum plate + engage module threads without bottoming out.

| Aluminum thickness | Screw length | Thread engagement | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3mm plate | M3 x 8mm | 5mm | At limit — risky if plate is slightly thick |
| 3mm plate | M3 x 6mm | 3mm | Adequate; 3mm engagement is fine for M3 |
| 5mm plate | M3 x 8mm | 3mm | Works |
| 5mm plate | M3 x 10mm | 5mm | At hard limit — do not exceed |

**Practical rule:** Screw length = plate thickness + 3–4mm of thread engagement. Never exceed 5mm engagement into the module. For a typical 3–5mm aluminum plate, **M3 x 6mm or M3 x 8mm** are the safest choices. M3 x 4mm is only appropriate for very thin plates (1–2mm). M3 x 5mm will give 0mm engagement through a 5mm plate.

> The user's prompt mentions M3 x 4mm or M3 x 5mm — both are listed below as options, but see note above about plate thickness.

## Product 4 — Thermal Compound (Heatsink Interface)

The ICEpower 1200AS2 datasheet and diyAudio community both confirm that thermal paste (or a thermal pad) should be used between the module baseplate and the aluminum plate. The ICEpower ASP series manual states the metal base is also the heatsink and should make firm contact with the case. Thermal compound fills microscopic surface irregularities and dramatically reduces interface thermal resistance.

**Important:** Use non-electrically-conductive paste. The module baseplate is live at chassis ground — conductive silver-based paste that squeezes out onto PCB areas could cause issues. Standard CPU-type pastes (carbon/zinc oxide-based) are safe.

## Torque and Assembly Notes

- **Max torque:** 0.5 Nm per ICEpower spec — this is very light. Use a torque driver or a short 2.5mm Allen key with fingertip force only. A standard full-length hex key makes it easy to over-torque.
- **Torque driver recommendation:** Search Amazon for "0.5 Nm torque screwdriver set" if doing this repeatedly across 90 screws. Otherwise use a short stubby hex key and a feel-based approach.
- **Tightening pattern:** Follow a star/cross pattern (like lug nuts) across the 18-hole grid to ensure even contact pressure across the full baseplate.
- **Surface prep:** Aluminum plate should be flat (machine-ground preferred) and clean. Degrease with isopropyl alcohol before applying thermal paste.
- **Thermal paste coverage:** Thin uniform layer across full baseplate area. A credit card or the included spatula works for spreading. Aim for ~0.1mm thickness — enough to fill surface irregularities, not so much it oozes out under clamping.

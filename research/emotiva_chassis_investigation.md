# Forum Investigation: Emotiva Rack-Mount Amplifier Chassis — Empty Purchase & DIY Alternatives

**Status:** RESEARCH
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Evidence volume:** Moderate — 25+ sources consulted across diyAudio, ASR, AVS Forum, HiFi2000/ModuShop, GhentAudio, AVNirvana, Audioholics, eBay

---

## Context

Emotiva makes rack-mount amplifiers (BasX A-300/A-500/A-700, XPA Gen3 series) in 2U 19" steel chassis with milled aluminum faceplates. The question is: can these or equivalent chassis be bought empty to house 5× ICEpower 1200AS2 modules (each 300mm × 100mm × 63mm)?

---

## Emotiva Chassis — What They Actually Are

### Construction
- **Material:** Heavy steel chassis body + milled aluminum faceplate (10mm). NOT full aluminum.
- **BasX A-300:** 17" × 4" × 15.5" deep (432mm × 102mm × 394mm). 2RU.
- **BasX A-500 / A-700:** 17" × 4" × 15.5"–16" deep. 2RU. Same form factor.
- **XPA Gen3 series:** 4RU steel chassis, milled aluminum faceplate.
- **Internal depth:** ~15–16" (381–406mm) usable. Some is consumed by rear connector panel.
- **Manufacturing:** All products designed and manufactured in China under Jade Design OEM oversight. Not a generic chassis — Emotiva works to their own tooled design, confirmed by Sound & Vision and HiFiVision forums.

### Is it Proprietary?
Yes. Emotiva's chassis is custom-tooled for their product line. While the manufacturing is outsourced to China (consistent with findings on AVS Forum "An Emotiva by any other name..." thread), there is no evidence the chassis is a widely-distributed generic mold available through standard Chinese OEM channels. The front-panel mill work, specific internal standoff positions, and rear connector cutouts are Emotiva-specific.

## Pro Arguments (Emotiva Chassis Availability)

- **eBay has used Emotiva amplifiers for salvage:** Complete units (BasX A-500, XPA-4, etc.) sell used for $200–400. A dead unit could yield a usable chassis. [eBay Emotiva Power Amplifiers](https://www.ebay.com/b/Emotiva-Home-Audio-Power-Amplifiers/14970/bn_73239522)
- **Emotiva Lounge "Emporium" section:** Users occasionally sell surplus Emotiva equipment including chassis components. [The Emotiva Lounge Emporium](https://emotivalounge.proboards.com/board/12/emporium)
- **Internal depth is workable:** ~15" usable depth can accommodate the 1200AS2's 300mm (11.8") board length with margin.
- **2U height is 88mm:** The 1200AS2 is 63mm tall, which fits within 2U (88.9mm) with about 25mm headroom — acceptable with low-profile standoffs.

## Con Arguments (Emotiva Chassis Availability)

- **Emotiva does not sell empty chassis.** Their accessories page offers only rack ears and silver trim. No chassis, no blanks, no enclosure kits. [Emotiva Accessories](https://emotiva.com/collections/accessories)
- **No empty chassis found on eBay.** Search for "Emotiva chassis", "Emotiva enclosure", "Emotiva case" empty yields zero results. Only complete amplifiers appear.
- **No parts store.** Emotiva's service page routes to a repair inquiry form — not a parts storefront. The Lounge "parts" thread returns a forum error (thread deleted/moved). [Emotiva Service](https://emotiva.com/pages/service-repair-inquiry)
- **Proprietary tooling:** Confirmed OEM/custom design. Not a standard generic Chinese chassis that can be re-ordered without an Emotiva relationship.
- **Steel chassis adds weight with no thermal benefit:** The Emotiva chassis is steel, not aluminum — meaning it does not contribute to the ICEpower 1200AS2's heatsinking, which relies on conduction to an aluminum mounting surface or chassis floor.
- **Salvage is not reliable:** Dead Emotiva units are rare enough that you cannot count on finding a clean chassis on-demand. This is not a repeatable procurement path.

## The Real Answer: Established DIY Rack Chassis Ecosystem

The DIY audio community has a well-developed ecosystem for exactly this use case. Emotiva's chassis is the wrong starting point.

### Tier 1: HiFi2000 / ModuShop (Italy — community consensus favorite)
- **What it is:** Italian manufacturer, 40+ years making audio enclosures. Community gold standard on diyAudio Chassis Discussion thread (200+ pages).
- **Relevant product:** **Dissipante 2U, 400mm depth** — confirmed by diyAudio forum posters as suitable for 2× ICEpower 1200AS2 modules. Price ~€185 for this size.
- **External heatsink fins:** The Dissipante has integrated side heatsink fins ("fireplace effect"), appropriate for the 1200AS2's thermal requirements.
- **19-inch rack compatible:** 483mm front panel, standard rack holes.
- **Internal width between heatsinks:** 360mm usable.
- **Note:** At 400mm depth with two 1200AS2 modules fitting, five modules would require at least 3–4U height or a custom wider chassis. The 2U/400mm fits 2 modules; 5 modules need more volume.
- **Sources:** [diyAudio Chassis Discussion](https://www.diyaudio.com/community/threads/chassis-discussion.221218/), [HiFi2000 Dissipante catalog](https://hifi2000.shop/site/en/catalog/dissipante), [diyAudio HiFi2000 thread](https://www.diyaudio.com/community/threads/hifi-2000-audio-enclosures.98537/)

### Tier 2: GhentAudio (China — ICEpower-specific, well regarded)
- **What it is:** GhentAudio makes purpose-built case kits specifically for ICEpower modules, including the 1200AS2.
- **D365a-S2:** Stereo (1× 1200AS2) case kit
- **D365a-Q1:** 4-channel (2× 1200AS2) case kit
- These are NOT 19" rack format — they are desktop enclosures. No rack-mount option confirmed for the 1200AS2 line.
- **BUT:** The 2U rackmount (for 1200AS2 + 2× 200AS2 in 200mm depth) shows rack interest.
- **Sources:** [GhentAudio ICEpower case list](https://www.ghentaudio.com/pages/icepower-case-list), [D365a-Q1 4CH case](https://ghentaudio.com/products/d365a-q1)

### Tier 3: Generic Chinese Rack Chassis (AliExpress/Yongu)
- **Yongu Case:** Offers 2U 19" rack chassis in aluminum, customizable depth. Stock models exist; custom orders available. [2U DIY Rackmount 19"](https://yongucase.com/products/2u-diy-rackmount-19-chassis)
- **AliExpress:** 19" rack chassis available at $30–80 in various depths. Quality inconsistent per forum reports ("good luck with cases from China" — ASR forum, single user report).
- **Par Metal Products (USA):** All-aluminum EMI-shielded rack chassis. Heavy, well-made, available on eBay. [Par Metal 2U eBay](https://www.ebay.com/itm/224604635362)
- **Hammond Manufacturing:** RMC series, 19" rack, standard depths up to 330mm. Doesn't reach 400mm standard depth. [Hammond RMC Series](https://www.hammfg.com/electronics/small-case/rack-mount/rmc)

### Tier 4: Apollon Audio (pre-built multichannel ICEpower)
- **Apollon makes 4ch (AS2400), 6ch (AS3600), 8ch (AS4800) amplifiers using 1200AS2 modules** in custom chassis. These are complete amplifiers, not empty chassis.
- AS1200 stereo unit: 450 × 305 × 185mm (h=104mm with feet) — desktop, not rack.
- Multi-channel units appear to be larger desktop enclosures, not 19" rack.
- **Relevant as a buy-vs-build datapoint**, not a chassis supplier.
- [Apollon AS4800](https://apollonaudio.com/product/as4800-icepower-based-class-d-8-channel-amplifier/)

## 5-Module Geometry Analysis

The user's system needs 5× ICEpower 1200AS2 modules (300mm × 100mm × 63mm each).

Layout options:
1. **Side-by-side in a wide chassis:** 5 modules × 100mm = 500mm minimum internal width. Standard 19" rack has ~430mm usable internal width — this does NOT fit all 5 side-by-side.
2. **Two rows (stacked):** 2 rows × 63mm = 126mm internal height minimum, plus PCB standoffs. This requires 3U (132mm) or 4U (177mm).
3. **Side-by-side in pairs + 1:** A 4U chassis with 2 rows of 2–3 modules is plausible. Internal floor area ~430mm × 380mm could hold 4 modules (2×2 layout) comfortably; 5th module requires vertical stacking.
4. **Separate chassis:** 2ch + 3ch split across two 2U units. Simpler thermally and mechanically.

**Key thermal note:** ICEpower 1200AS2 modules must mount to an aluminum surface for conduction cooling. A steel chassis (like Emotiva's) does not conduct heat effectively from the module mounting feet — an aluminum chassis floor or dedicated heatsink plate is required. This rules out steel chassis entirely.

## Bottom Line

**Emotiva chassis:** Not purchasable empty. Not recommended even if it were — it's steel (thermally wrong for ICEpower) and not designed for custom module mounting.

**Best DIY rack path for 5× 1200AS2:**
1. **Two-chassis approach** (recommended): Split into 2× stereo (2ch) + 1× mono or a 4ch+1ch split. Use HiFi2000 Dissipante 2U 400mm for the larger chassis (~€185 each) — confirmed community choice for 1200AS2 builds. Thermally better, easier to build, serviceable.
2. **Single large chassis approach:** Custom or larger HiFi2000 chassis (3U/4U) with aluminum floor plate and fan cooling. More complex; few documented builds at this scale.
3. **Ghent Audio case kits:** Purpose-built for 1200AS2 but desktop format. Not rack.
4. **Buy a complete Apollon multichannel amp** and skip the chassis problem entirely.

## Design Notes

### Why 8 cu ft?
- Provides ~4 cu ft per driver (good match for UMII18-22)
- Qtc 0.95 — slightly elevated but easily corrected by Dirac
- Compact footprint (4.9 sq ft) vs 6.0 sq ft for 12 cu ft design
- 30-35 lb weight savings vs larger designs

### Why Plywood?
- ~34 lbs lighter than equivalent MDF build
- Similar cost to MDF
- Under 200 lbs total — solid 2-person lift
- Requires sealing but adds only ~30 min to build

### Why Enhanced Bracing?
- Plywood has weaker edge joints than MDF
- Corner braces compensate for this weakness
- Full rib + cross-brace system ensures panel rigidity
- No acoustic compromise vs MDF construction

## Comparison to Original 12 cu ft Design

| Parameter | 8 cu ft | 12 cu ft | Difference |
|-----------|---------|----------|------------|
| External size | 26.5" cube | 29.5" cube | -3" each dimension |
| Footprint | 4.9 sq ft | 6.0 sq ft | -18% |
| Weight (plywood) | 203 lbs | 264 lbs | -61 lbs |
| Qtc | 0.95 | 0.83 | +0.12 |
| F3 (in-room) | ~22 Hz | ~21 Hz | +1 Hz |
| SPL @ 20 Hz | 121 dB | 123 dB | -2 dB |

The 8 cu ft design trades ~2 dB of output and slightly higher Qtc for significantly reduced size and weight. After Dirac correction, the two designs would sound virtually identical.

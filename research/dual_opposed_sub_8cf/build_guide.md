# 8 cu ft Dual-Opposed Subwoofer - Build Guide

## Overview

This guide covers assembly of an 8 cu ft dual-opposed subwoofer using big box 3/4" plywood with enhanced bracing.

**Estimated Build Time:** 12-16 hours active work + curing time  
**Skill Level:** Intermediate woodworking  
**Tools Required:** Circular saw, jigsaw or router, drill, clamps

---

## Before You Begin

### Critical Notes for Plywood Construction

1. **SEAL ALL INTERIOR SURFACES** — Plywood has voids that leak air
2. **Cut face-up** — Reduces tearout on visible surfaces
3. **Label everything** — Mark interior/exterior faces on all panels
4. **Check for square** — Measure diagonals during assembly
5. **Use generous glue** — Plywood edges don't absorb glue like MDF

---

## Step 1: Cut All Panels

**Time: 2-3 hours**

Cut all panels per the cut list. Use a circular saw with a fine-tooth blade (80+ teeth) for clean cuts.

### Shell Panels
| Panel | Qty | Size |
|-------|-----|------|
| Top | 1 | 26.5" × 26.5" |
| Bottom (outer) | 1 | 26.5" × 26.5" |
| Bottom (inner) | 1 | 26.5" × 26.5" |
| Front/Back | 2 | 25" × 24.5" |
| Left/Right | 2 | 25" × 24.5" |

### Bracing
| Component | Qty | Size |
|-----------|-----|------|
| Cross-Brace A | 2 | 25" × 4" |
| Cross-Brace B | 2 | 25" × 4" |
| Rib Braces | 16 | 1.5" × 24.5" |
| Corner Braces | 4 | 3" × 24.5" |

---

## Step 2: Cut Driver Holes

**Time: 1 hour**

Cut 16.875" diameter holes in top panel and both bottom panels.

1. Mark center point (13.25" from all edges)
2. Use router with circle jig, or drill pilot and use jigsaw
3. Sand edges smooth
4. Drill 8 bolt holes on 17.75" diameter circle
   - Use 9/32" drill bit for 1/4-20 bolt clearance
   - Space evenly at 45° intervals

**TIP:** Stack and clamp the two bottom panels together, cut both holes simultaneously for perfect alignment.

---

## Step 3: Laminate Bottom Panels

**Time: 30 min + 24 hour cure**

The doubled bottom panel (1.5" total) supports the inverted driver.

1. Dry-fit the two bottom panels, verify hole alignment
2. Apply even coat of Titebond III to mating faces
3. Align carefully — holes must match perfectly
4. Clamp with cauls across entire surface
5. Let cure 24 hours minimum

---

## Step 4: Install T-Nuts

**Time: 30 minutes**

Install T-nuts from the INSIDE face of each driver panel.

### Top Panel
- 8× T-nuts (1/4-20)
- Hammer in from interior face
- Use backup block behind panel

### Bottom Panel (Laminated)
- 8× T-nuts (1/4-20)
- Install into TOP layer only (interior face)
- Holes go through both layers, T-nuts seat in top layer

**CRITICAL:** T-nuts must be installed before final assembly — you cannot access them later.

---

## Step 5: Cut Brace Notches

**Time: 30 minutes**

Cut interlocking notches in cross-braces.

### Cross-Brace A (×2)
- Notch from TOP edge
- 4" wide × 2" deep
- Centered on brace length

### Cross-Brace B (×2)
- Notch from BOTTOM edge
- 4" wide × 2" deep
- Centered on brace length

```
A braces: notch on top      B braces: notch on bottom
    ┌────┬────┬────┐            ┌────┴────┴────┐
    │    │    │    │            │    │    │    │
    └────┴────┴────┘            └────┬────┬────┘
```

When interlocked, they form a + shape with the notches mating at center.

---

## Step 6: Seal Interior Surfaces

**Time: 1-2 hours + overnight dry**

**THIS STEP IS CRITICAL FOR PLYWOOD**

Plywood has internal voids that will leak air, reducing bass output and driver control.

### Procedure
1. Mix Titebond III with 10-20% water (thin consistency)
2. Brush or roll onto ALL interior panel faces:
   - Inside faces of all 6 shell panels
   - All faces of all bracing pieces
3. Pay extra attention to:
   - Cut edges (end grain absorbs more)
   - Plywood edge grain
   - Any visible voids
4. Let dry completely (4+ hours, overnight preferred)
5. Apply second coat if surface looks porous

### Alternative Sealers
- Shellac (fast drying, 1-2 hours)
- Polyurethane (very durable)
- Latex paint (cheap, effective)

---

## Step 7: Attach Rib Braces to Side Panels

**Time: 1-2 hours**

Glue vertical rib braces to interior face of all 4 side panels.

### Position
- 2 ribs per panel
- Located at 6.25" and 18.75" from panel edges
- This divides the 25" width into: 6.25" | 12.5" | 6.25"

### Procedure
1. Mark rib positions on all 4 side panels
2. Apply glue to rib (full length)
3. Position and clamp, or use pin nails to hold
4. Ensure ribs are parallel to panel edges
5. Let glue set before moving (30 min minimum)

---

## Step 8: Prepare Corner Braces

**Time: 30 minutes**

Corner braces reinforce the weaker plywood edge joints.

### Option 1: L-Shaped (Best)
1. Cut 3" × 24.5" strips
2. Rip a 2.25" × 0.75" strip from one edge
3. Glue back at 90° to form L-profile
4. Or: cut two 1.5" × 24.5" strips and glue at 90°

### Option 2: Simple Strip
Use 3" × 24.5" strips as-is, glue flat to one wall in each corner

---

## Step 9: Cut Terminal Plate Hole

**Time: 15 minutes**

Cut the terminal plate opening in the **back panel** before assembly.

### Location
- **Horizontal:** Centered (12.5" from either side edge)
- **Vertical:** 5" from bottom edge of panel

### Procedure
1. Mark centerpoint at 12.5" from side, 5" from bottom
2. Trace terminal plate outline (typically 3.5" W × 2.5" H)
3. Drill 1/4" relief holes at corners
4. Cut opening with jigsaw
5. File/sand edges smooth
6. Test fit terminal plate

```
BACK PANEL (exterior view)
┌─────────────────────────┐
│                         │
│                         │
│                         │
│                         │
│      ┌─────────┐        │  ← Terminal plate
│      │ ○     ○ │        │     5" from bottom
│      └─────────┘        │     centered
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Do this BEFORE assembly** — much easier to cut with panel lying flat.

---

## Step 10: Assemble Side Frame

**Time: 1-2 hours**

Assemble the 4 side panels into a box (no top/bottom yet).

### Assembly Order
1. Stand Front panel on edge
2. Apply glue to Left panel edge
3. Attach Left panel to Front (forms corner)
4. Apply glue, attach Back panel
5. Apply glue, attach Right panel to complete box
6. Use screws or pin nails to hold while glue sets

### Critical Checks
- Measure diagonals — must be equal (±1/8")
- Check for square with framing square
- Adjust before glue sets

**Box dimensions should be:** 25" × 25" (internal)

---

## Step 11: Install Cross-Braces

**Time: 1 hour**

Install the interlocking cross-braces at two levels.

### Upper Level
- Position: 9.5" down from top edge of side frame
- Insert Brace A (front-to-back)
- Insert Brace B (left-to-right)
- Interlock at center notches
- Glue all joints:
  - Brace ends to side panels
  - The interlocking notch joint

### Lower Level
- Position: 9.5" up from bottom edge of side frame
- Same procedure as upper level

```
Side view of cabinet:
    ┌─────────────────────┐ ← Top edge
    │                     │
    │ ═══════════════════ │ ← Upper cross-braces (9.5" from top)
    │                     │
    │                     │
    │                     │
    │ ═══════════════════ │ ← Lower cross-braces (9.5" from bottom)
    │                     │
    └─────────────────────┘ ← Bottom edge
```

---

## Step 12: Install Corner Braces

**Time: 30 minutes**

Glue corner braces into all 4 interior corners, full height.

1. Apply glue to corner brace contact surfaces
2. Press into corner
3. Use pin nails or clamps to hold
4. Repeat for all 4 corners

---

## Step 13: Attach Bottom Panel

**Time: 30 minutes**

Attach the laminated (doubled) bottom panel.

1. Apply glue to bottom edges of side frame
2. Position bottom panel (T-nuts facing UP/interior)
3. Check alignment — edges should be flush
4. Secure with 2" screws every 4-6"
5. Screws go through bottom panel into side panel edges

**Use 2" screws** — the bottom panel is 1.5" thick, screws must reach side panel.

---

## Step 14: Attach Top Panel

**Time: 30 minutes**

Attach the top panel.

1. Apply glue to top edges of side frame
2. Position top panel (T-nuts facing DOWN/interior)
3. Check alignment — edges should be flush
4. Secure with 1.5" screws every 4-6"

**Use 1.5" screws** — the top panel is 0.75" thick.

---

## Step 15: Seal Interior Joints

**Time: 30 minutes**

Run silicone caulk along all interior corners and joints.

1. Use a caulk gun with silicone sealant
2. Run bead along:
   - All 4 vertical corners
   - Top panel to side panel joints (inside)
   - Bottom panel to side panel joints (inside)
   - Around cross-brace ends
3. Smooth with finger or caulk tool
4. Let cure per manufacturer instructions

---

## Step 16: Attach Legs

**Time: 30 minutes**

Attach 4 hardwood corner legs.

### Leg Specifications
- Size: 3.5" × 3.5" × 4" tall
- Material: Oak, maple, or poplar
- Position: Inset 0.5" from cabinet edges

### Procedure
1. Position leg at corner
2. Drill pilot holes from INSIDE cabinet
3. Secure with 4× #10 × 2.5" screws per leg
4. Attach rubber isolation pad to bottom of each leg

---

## Step 17: Install Terminal Plate

**Time: 10 minutes**

1. Apply foam gasket tape around terminal plate opening (exterior)
2. Insert terminal plate from outside
3. Secure with provided screws
4. Connect internal wiring leads (leave enough slack to reach both drivers)

---

## Step 18: Mount Drivers

**Time: 30 minutes**

### Top Driver (Cone UP)
1. Apply gasket tape around cutout
2. Position driver (cone facing up, into room)
3. Insert 1/4-20 × 2" bolts through driver flange
4. Thread into T-nuts
5. Torque in star pattern

### Bottom Driver (Cone DOWN)
1. Apply gasket tape around cutout
2. Position driver (cone facing down, toward floor)
3. Insert 1/4-20 × 2.5" bolts (longer for doubled panel)
4. Thread into T-nuts
5. Torque in star pattern

---

## Step 19: Wire and Test

**Time: 30 minutes**

### Wiring (Series Connection)
1. Connect wire from terminal (+) to top driver (+)
2. Connect top driver (-) to bottom driver (+)
3. Connect bottom driver (-) to terminal (-)
4. Total impedance: 4Ω

```
Terminal (+) ────→ Top Driver (+)
                   Top Driver (-) ────→ Bottom Driver (+)
Terminal (-) ←──────────────────────── Bottom Driver (-)
```

### Phase Check
Both drivers wired + to + through the chain = IN PHASE
- Cones move in OPPOSITE directions simultaneously
- This provides force cancellation

### Air Seal Test
1. Connect to amp at low volume
2. Play low frequency tone (30-40 Hz)
3. Feel around all joints for air leaks
4. Re-caulk any leaks found

## Advantages of Each Material

### Baltic Birch Advantages

- **~24 lbs lighter** (easier to move, less floor stress)
- **Better screw holding** in panel edges
- **Superior dimensional stability** (less seasonal expansion)
- **More moisture resistant** (better for basement installations)
- **Higher stiffness-to-weight ratio** (potentially less panel resonance)
- **Attractive edge appearance** when finished (visible plies)

### MDF Advantages

- **Lower cost** (~$100 savings on materials)
- **Easier to cut** without tear-out
- **Uniform density** (no voids or grain variations)
- **Excellent paint surface** (smooth, consistent finish)
- **Better acoustic damping** (higher internal losses)
- **Easier to rout** (clean edges without splintering)

---

## Recommendation

For a permanently corner-loaded subwoofer:

**MDF is the practical choice.** The ~24 lb weight difference (260 vs 237 lbs) is modest when the cabinet already requires 2 people to move regardless of material. The cost savings of ~$100 can be better spent on amplification or acoustic treatment.

**Baltic Birch makes sense if:**

- You need to move it frequently
- Building multiple units (cumulative weight adds up)
- High-humidity environment (basement without climate control)
- You want visible plywood edges as a design element
- Edge screw holding is critical (less need for T-nuts)

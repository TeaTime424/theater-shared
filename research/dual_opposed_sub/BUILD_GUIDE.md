# Dual-Opposed Subwoofer Build Guide

## Complete Assembly Instructions

**Version:** 2.0  
**Configuration:** Dual-opposed vertical with interlocking cross-braces and rib braces  
**Drivers:** 2× Dayton Audio UMII18-22  

---

## Build Overview

| Step | Description | Time Estimate |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | Cut all MDF panels | 2-3 hours |
| 2 | Laminate bottom panels | 30 min + 24hr cure |
| 3 | Cut driver holes & drill bolt holes | 1-2 hours |
| 4 | Cut notches in cross-braces | 30 min |
| 5 | Glue rib braces to side panels | 1 hour + 1hr cure |
| 6 | Assemble side frame | 1-2 hours |
| 7 | Install cross-braces | 1 hour |
| 8 | Attach top & bottom panels | 1-2 hours |
| 9 | Attach corner legs | 30 min |
| 10 | Mount drivers & wiring | 1 hour |

**Total estimated time:** 10-14 hours active work + curing time

## Step 1: Cut All MDF Panels

### Sheet Layout

**Sheet 1:**
- TOP: 29.5" × 29.5"
- BOTTOM #1: 29.5" × 29.5"
- SIDE 1: 27.25" × 28"
- BRACE 1: 28" × 4"
- BRACE 2: 28" × 4"

**Sheet 2:**
- BOTTOM #2: 29.5" × 29.5"
- SIDE 2: 27.25" × 28"
- SIDE 3: 27.25" × 28"
- SIDE 4: 27.25" × 28"
- BRACE 3: 28" × 4"
- BRACE 4: 28" × 4"

**From Scraps:**
- 16× RIB BRACES: 27.25" × 1.5"

### Cutting Tips

1. Use a track saw or table saw with 80+ tooth blade for clean cuts
2. Cut panels slightly oversized (+1/16"), then trim to exact dimensions
3. Label each panel immediately after cutting (TOP, BOT1, BOT2, SIDE1-4, etc.)
4. Stack matching panels to verify dimensions match
5. Save all MDF scraps for rib braces

---

## Step 2: Laminate Bottom Panels

The bottom panel must be doubled to 1.5" thickness to securely support the inverted 47 lb driver.

### Procedure

1. Lay BOTTOM #1 on flat surface
2. Apply Titebond III liberally in zigzag pattern (full coverage when spread)
3. Carefully align BOTTOM #2 on top, edges flush
4. Clamp every 6" in grid pattern
5. Wipe squeeze-out immediately with damp cloth
6. **CURE 24 HOURS MINIMUM** - do not disturb

### Critical Notes

- Do NOT cut the driver hole yet - cut after lamination
- Ensure panels are perfectly aligned before clamping
- Use wax paper under assembly to prevent gluing to work surface

---

## Step 3: Cut Driver Holes & Drill Bolt Holes

### Driver Cutout (16.875" diameter)

1. Mark center point on TOP panel
2. Mark center point on laminated BOTTOM panel
3. Use router with jasper circle jig set to 8.4375" radius (half of 16.875")
4. Cut through TOP panel (¾")
5. Cut through BOTTOM panel (both layers at once = 1.5")

**Cutting through both bottom layers together ensures perfect alignment.**

### Bolt Holes

1. Create template with 8 holes on bolt circle (use driver as template)
2. Mark 8 bolt positions on both panels
3. Drill ¼" holes through each panel
4. Countersink from outside if desired

### T-Nut Installation

1. Turn panels so INSIDE face is up
2. Position T-nut over each hole
3. Hammer prongs into MDF until flange is flush
4. Verify T-nut threads are accessible from outside

---

## Step 4: Cut Notches in Cross-Braces

Each pair of cross-braces interlocks at the center with half-depth notches.

### Notch Dimensions

- Width: 4" (matches brace width)
- Depth: 2" (half of brace width)
- Location: Centered on 28" length (at 14" mark)

### Cutting Pattern

| Brace | Notch Position |
|-------|----------------|
| Upper Brace A | Notch from TOP |
| Upper Brace B | Notch from BOTTOM |
| Lower Brace A | Notch from TOP |
| Lower Brace B | Notch from BOTTOM |

### Procedure

1. Mark center of each brace (14" from either end)
2. Mark notch width: 2" each side of center = 4" total
3. Set table saw blade height to exactly 2"
4. Make multiple passes to remove waste
5. Clean up with chisel if needed
6. Test-fit pairs - they should interlock snugly with tops flush

---

## Step 5: Glue Rib Braces to Side Panels

Two rib braces per side panel, positioned at ¼ and ¾ of panel width.

### Rib Positions

- Panel width: 28"
- Rib 1: 7" from left edge
- Rib 2: 21" from left edge (7" from right edge)
- This creates sections: 7" | ~12.5" | 7"

### Procedure

1. Mark rib positions on all 4 side panels
2. Apply glue to rib edge (the 27.25" × ¾" face)
3. Position rib on panel interior face, aligned with marks
4. Clamp every 6-8" along length
5. Verify rib is perpendicular to panel surface
6. Wipe excess glue
7. Allow 30-60 minutes before handling
8. Repeat for all 16 ribs (4 panels × 2 ribs each = 8 ribs per brace level)

### Note on Rib Orientation

Ribs protrude 1.5" from panel surface into the cabinet interior. They will not intersect with the cross-braces which run through the cabinet center.

---

## Step 6: Assemble Side Frame

Join the 4 side panels into a square frame (27.25" tall × 28" × 28" internal).

### Assembly Order

1. Lay SIDE 1 flat on work surface, ribs facing up
2. Apply glue to one 27.25" edge
3. Position SIDE 2 perpendicular, forming 90° corner
4. Drill pilot holes through SIDE 2 into edge of SIDE 1
5. Drive #8 × 1.5" screws every 6"
6. Check corner is square with framing square
7. Repeat to attach SIDE 3 and SIDE 4
8. Verify all corners are 90° before glue sets

### Critical Notes

- Use corner clamps or have helper hold panels square
- The frame must be perfectly square or top/bottom panels won't fit
- Allow 30 minutes for glue to set before moving

---

## Step 7: Install Cross-Braces

Two levels of interlocking cross-braces: upper (9.5" from top) and lower (9.5" from bottom).

### Brace Positions

| Level | Distance from Edge | Measurement Point |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Upper | 9.5" from TOP edge | Inside face of top panel |
| Lower | 9.5" from BOTTOM edge | Inside face of bottom panel |

### Procedure

1. Measure and mark 9.5" from top edge on all 4 interior walls
2. Dry-fit interlocked upper braces, check fit to walls
3. Apply glue to:
   - Both ends of each brace (wall contact)
   - The notch joint where braces interlock
4. Insert braces into frame
5. Interlock at center
6. Clamp or screw ends to walls (#8 × 1.5" screws)
7. Verify braces are level
8. Repeat for lower brace level

### Important

- Glue the notch joint - this dramatically increases rigidity
- Braces must be level for proper driver alignment

---

## Step 8: Attach Top & Bottom Panels

### Top Panel (0.75" single)

1. Apply continuous bead of glue to top edges of side frame
2. Position TOP panel with driver hole centered
3. Clamp or weight down, check alignment
4. Drill pilot holes through TOP into side panel edges
5. Drive #8 × 1.5" screws every 4-6" around perimeter

### Bottom Panel (1.5" doubled)

1. Flip cabinet so top is on work surface (protect with padding)
2. Apply glue to bottom edges of side frame
3. Position BOTTOM panel with driver hole centered
4. Clamp or weight, check alignment
5. Drill pilot holes
6. Drive #8 × 2" screws every 4-6" (longer screws for thicker panel)

---

## Step 9: Attach Corner Legs

### Leg Specifications

- Material: Hardwood (oak, maple, or similar)
- Size: 3.5" × 3.5" × 4" tall
- Position: Inset 0.5" from cabinet edges

### Procedure

1. Flip cabinet so BOTTOM is facing up
2. Mark leg positions at all 4 corners (0.5" inset from edges)
3. Pre-drill from inside of cabinet (through bottom panel into leg position)
4. Apply glue to leg top surface
5. Position leg
6. Drive 4× #10 × 2.5" screws per leg from inside
7. Repeat for all 4 legs
8. Attach rubber isolation pads to leg bottoms

---

## Step 10: Mount Drivers

### Preparation

1. Apply foam gasket tape around driver cutout on both panels
2. Identify TOP and BOTTOM positions

### Top Driver Installation

1. Position driver in TOP cutout with cone facing UP
2. Align bolt holes
3. Insert 8× ¼-20 × 2" bolts through driver flange
4. Thread into T-nuts finger-tight
5. Tighten in star pattern (opposite pairs)
6. Do not over-tighten

### Bottom Driver Installation

1. Carefully flip cabinet (have helper support weight)
2. Position driver in BOTTOM cutout with cone facing DOWN
3. Align bolt holes
4. Insert 8× ¼-20 × 2.5" bolts (longer for thicker panel)
5. Thread into T-nuts finger-tight
6. Tighten in star pattern
7. Do not over-tighten

### Wiring

**CRITICAL: Both drivers must be wired IN PHASE**

When one cone moves outward (pushes), the other must move inward (pulls). This cancels reaction forces at the cabinet.

1. Connect positive (+) terminal of amp to positive terminal of Driver 1
2. Connect positive terminal of Driver 1 to positive terminal of Driver 2
3. Connect negative (-) terminals the same way (series) OR
4. Wire in parallel if amp supports the impedance

**Resulting impedance:**
- Series: 8Ω (4Ω + 4Ω)
- Parallel: 2Ω (4Ω || 4Ω)

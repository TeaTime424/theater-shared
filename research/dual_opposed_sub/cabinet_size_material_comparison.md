## Material Trade-offs

### MDF
**Pros:**
- Perfectly uniform density
- Excellent machining (smooth cuts, no tearout)
- Great paint surface
- No voids — naturally airtight
- Predictable acoustic properties
- Proven in thousands of subwoofer builds

**Cons:**
- Heaviest option
- Poor moisture resistance
- Edges can crumble
- Dust is hazardous (wear mask)

### Big Box Plywood (CDX, Sanded Pine, Birch Veneer)
**Pros:**
- Lightest option (28% lighter than MDF)
- Similar cost to MDF
- Good screw holding in face grain
- More impact resistant than MDF
- Widely available

**Cons:**
- Voids in interior plies (potential air leaks)
- Inconsistent density
- Poor edge screw holding
- Requires sealing all interior surfaces
- Surface may need filling before paint
- Can warp if not stored flat

### Baltic Birch
**Pros:**
- Void-free construction
- Excellent screw holding everywhere
- Strong and stiff
- Beautiful edges (can be finished exposed)
- Best moisture resistance
- Good balance of weight and quality

**Cons:**
- Expensive (~2× MDF price)
- Limited availability (specialty lumber yards)
- 5'×5' sheets (not standard 4'×8')

---

## Sealing Requirements for Big Box Plywood

If using standard plywood, seal interior surfaces before assembly:

1. Thin Titebond III 10-20% with water
2. Brush or roll onto all interior panel faces
3. Let dry completely (2-4 hours)
4. Assemble as normal with generous glue on all joints
5. Optional: caulk interior corners after assembly

This adds ~30 minutes active time plus drying.

## Recommendations

### Best Balance (Recommended)
**8 cu ft, Big Box Plywood: 199 lbs**
- Under 200 lbs — solid 2-person lift
- Qtc 0.95 — easily corrected by Dirac
- ~$100 materials, widely available
- Seal interior surfaces during build

### Lightest Option
**7 cu ft, Big Box Plywood: 192 lbs**
- Lightest practical configuration
- Qtc 1.00 — right at the edge, Dirac required
- Most compact footprint (4.5 sq ft)

### Highest Quality
**8 cu ft, Baltic Birch: 214 lbs**
- Void-free, no sealing needed
- Beautiful if edges are exposed
- Premium build quality
- ~$200 materials

### Proven/Simple
**8 cu ft, MDF: 233 lbs**
- No sealing, no voids, predictable
- Standard subwoofer building material
- Heaviest, but still manageable with 2-3 people
